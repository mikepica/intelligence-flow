The Three Ingredients

Three Things That Create the Flow

Intelligence flow is not a system you install. It emerges from three things working together.

Your PAI, Your Expertise

Your Thinking, Amplified

Every person's AI is optimized with their experience, judgment, and methodology. It is not a generic chatbot.

It is your thinking, amplified. It knows how you work, what you have learned, and what matters to you.

It lives in your workflow: chat when you need it, embedded in your tools, proactive when it matters.

Skills — Shared Expertise

How Expertise Moves Between People

When Maria shares her Clinical Data Analysis skill, she is not sending a document. She is sharing her codified methodology.

Skills are shared tools, connective tissue between teams, and carriers of tribal knowledge.

When two people use complementary Skills, their work naturally aligns without anyone coordinating it.

Scorecard Goals

The Shared Compass

Goals from Enterprise down to Individual create a shared frame of reference for every person in the organization.

When everyone's work is connected to the same goal hierarchy, alignment happens automatically.

Progress rolls up naturally — not through reports, but through work.

Put these three together, and intelligence doesn't need to be pushed or pulled. It flows.

The Human Experience

What Changes

Not a system comparison. A change in how it feels to do your best work.

Today

You finish your analysis. You email it to your manager. They present it in a review meeting. Three weeks later, someone in another function discovers it was relevant to their work.

You don't know what leadership is prioritizing this quarter until it trickles down in a town hall. By then, you've already spent weeks on something that's no longer the top priority.

You have expertise no one else knows about because there's no way to make it visible. It lives in your head, used only when someone happens to ask.

With the Flow

You finish your analysis. It is already connected to the BU goal. The right people see it immediately. Someone in Commercial reaches out because their PAI flagged the relevance.

Leadership priorities flow to you in context — your PAI tells you exactly how a priority shift affects YOUR work, your deadlines, your deliverables.

Your expertise is codified in Skills that others can find, use, and build on. Your methodology outlives your memory. Others benefit from your years of experience.

The Leadership View

What Leadership Sees

The natural result when intelligence flows. Not because people filed reports — because work IS the report.

Real-time visibility into progress

Not because people filed reports, but because the work itself generates the signal. Every contribution is automatically connected to the goal it advances.

Cross-functional alignment, organically

No more coordination meetings to discover overlapping work. When Maria's analysis impacts David's model, the system surfaces it. Functions align because the intelligence flows between them.

Adjust priorities, see the impact cascade

When a VP shifts a priority, every PAI in the organization adapts. People see how the change affects their work. Leadership sees who is affected and how the organization responds.
