The Intelligence Gap

How siloed information is costing AstraZeneca speed, alignment, and strategic clarity

The Status Quo

The Current Reality

Across the enterprise, the way we track and report on strategic goals hasn't kept pace with the speed of the business.

Spreadsheet Goals

Annual goals live in static spreadsheets, disconnected from the daily work that drives them forward.

Slide-Based Reporting

Progress is compiled into PowerPoint decks for periodic reviews -- a snapshot frozen in time the moment it's created.

Stale Data

By the time leadership sees the data, it's already 2-4 weeks old. Decisions are made on yesterday's reality.

Manual Fire Drills

Every reporting cycle triggers a scramble -- pulling data, formatting slides, chasing approvals -- consuming weeks of productivity.

The Root Cause

The Silo Effect

Information is trapped within organizational boundaries. Knowledge can't flow where it's needed most.

The Impact

The Cost of Disconnection

Organizational silos don't just slow things down -- they create compounding strategic risk.

Weeks of Lag

Leadership decisions are based on data that's 2-4 weeks old. In a fast-moving environment, stale data means missed opportunities and delayed course corrections.

Invisible Expertise

Critical knowledge is trapped inside individuals, invisible to the broader organization. When people move or leave, institutional wisdom vanishes with them.

Strategic Drift

Teams are working hard -- but without real-time alignment signals, effort diverges from shifting priorities. Hard work doesn't guarantee strategic impact.
